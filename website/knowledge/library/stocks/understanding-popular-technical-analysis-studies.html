    Simple Moving Average

    One of the most popular indicators inÂ technical analysis, the Simple Moving Average (SMA), is a lagging indicator which calculates the average closing price of a stock over a given number of days. Like other moving average indicators, its aim is to determine the direction in which the price of the stock is moving and estimate its future direction, based on previous prices.

    • The SMA is calculated by adding previous closing prices and dividing the result by the number of time periods under consideration.
    • Power E*TRADE will use 50 days as the standard period. So, the SMA at any given time is the average of closing prices for the previous 50 days.
    • The 50-day period moves forward one day each day (hence the term â€œmovingâ€) and the SMA is plotted as a continuous line on the underlying securityâ€™s price chart.

    A trader may choose to use the SMA as an indicator of whether a securityâ€™s price will rise, or if it will reverse.     Itâ€™s important to note that because the SMA is based on the average of past prices, it may be of more limited use during fast-changing or sideways markets.

    Relative Strength Index

    The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a widely-used technical indicator which measures the speed and magnitude of the changes in a stockâ€™s price by comparing recent gains to recent losses. A lagging momentum oscillator, the RSI may help traders and investors spot potential trend reversal signals and strategize on entry and exit points.

    • To calculate this indicator, the average gains from a desired period are added, then divided by the average quantity. The resulting value is referred to as relative strength.
    • Power E*TRADE will default to a period of 14 days, though short-term traders may prefer a shorter period and long-term traders might prefer a longer one.

    The relative strength value is shown on a range from zero to 100, where a value above 70 indicates that the stock may be overbought (suggesting the potential for a price decline) and a value lower than 30 indicates a possible oversold condition (suggesting the potential for a price increase).Â  Given that the RSI is gauging the strength of price movements, traders frequently use it concurrently with other types of indicators such as moving averages or the MACD.

    This technical indicatorâ€”consisting of an MACD line, signal line, and a bar graph referred to as a histogramâ€”is often used to identify divergence between price and momentum in the market, which some traders may interpret as a signal of a possible reversal.
